The Renowned Country Singer Ken Curtis: His Life, Career, and Legacy

Singer’s Bio

Ken Curtis was an American actor, singer, and songwriter, born on July 2, 1916, in Lamar, Colorado. He began his career as a vocalist and radio performer with the big-band outfit Tommy Dorsey in the late 1930s. He then joined the legendary Sons of the Pioneers in 1949, where he replaced the group’s original lead singer, Len Slye (who later became famous as Roy Rogers). With this group, Curtis achieved popularity as a western singer and crooner.

Age

Ken Curtis died on April 28, 1991, at the age of 74.

Relationships and Children

Curtis was married three times, first to Lorraine Page from 1942 to 1950, and then to Torrie Connelly from 1952 to 1964. In 1966, he married his third wife, the artist and sculptor Barbara Ford. Curtis had three children, two with Connelly and one with Ford.

Height

Ken Curtis stood at an impressive height of 6 feet 2 inches.

Career

After his stint with the Sons of the Pioneers, Curtis landed acting roles in various films and television shows, including The Searchers (1956) and Gunsmoke (1959-75), in which he played the role of Festus Haggen. He also appeared in more than 50 films alongside notable actors such as John Wayne and Randolph Scott.

As a singer and songwriter, Ken Curtis recorded numerous albums, including “Lonesome Road,” “The Ken Curtis Collection,” and “The Utah Carol.” He was known for his rich baritone voice, which he used to great effect on songs such as “Tumbling Tumbleweeds” and “Cool Water.”

Ken Curtis is best known for his role as Festus Haggen on the long-running television series Gunsmoke.
