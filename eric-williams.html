Biography

Eric Williams was born in Trinidad And Tobago on September 25, 1911. From the early 1960s until his death, this People’s National Movement leader served as Trinidad and Tobago’s first Prime Minister. Also a scholar and author, he published an important 1944 work titled Capitalism and Slavery. A decade apart, Williams and Patrick Manning both served as Prime Minister of Trinidad and Tobago. He earned both his bachelor’s degree (in history) and his doctorate from St Catherine’s College, Oxford. He began his political career as a member of the Anglo-American Caribbean Commission.
