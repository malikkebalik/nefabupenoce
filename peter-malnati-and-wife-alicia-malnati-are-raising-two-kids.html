Peter Malnati And Wife Alicia Malnati Are Raising Two Kids

American golfer Peter Malnati is the father of two kids with his wife, Alicia Malnati. Alicia caddied for him on the Web.com Tour in 2015, no doubt, she is a supportive partner.

Aside from being a mother of two, she is a scholar & artist who runs her own website AliciaHMalnati.com, which focuses on newsletters.

Peter currently competes in the PGA tour. Previously, he played in the Korn Ferry Tour & became the two-time champion.

Malnati, at the University of Missouri, played college golf. Eventually, he turned professional in 2009.

Additionally, the only glory he got on the PGA Tour happened at the 2015 Sanderson Farms Championship. The golfer achieved victory in his seventh tournament at the News Sentinel Open in the season of 2013.

Furthermore, to achieve his PGA Tour card for 2014, Peter finished 18th on the 2013 Web.com.

Again, after losing his card, he returned to the Web.com Tour for the 2015 season. This season led him to win on the tour at the Brasil Champions.

Peter Malnati Wife: Alicia Malnati

Peter Malnati and Alicia Malnati exchanged their vows in 2013 after some time in their relationship. Some sources claim that they have been together for almost a decade.

After six years of marriage, they gave birth to their firstborn son in October 2019 and named him Hatcher Malnati.

The golfer & his wife welcomed their second child, son Dash Otis Malnati, on November 25, 2022.

Alicia Malnati (b. December 2, 1986), the daughter of Ward Allen and Connie Serrano, is a graduate of the University of Missouri (same as her husband). Alicia studied for a bachelor’s diploma in journalism.

Not only that, Peter’s wife studied for a Master’s in Educational Leadership and Policy Analysis.

Furthermore, she also earned a Ph.D. in educational, school, and counseling psychology.

Currently, she is working as a program assistant and researcher for the Center of Sport, Peace, and Society.

PGA Tour Meeting

The boards of directors of the PGA Tour met at Bay Hill Club and Lodge for more than seven hours.

Peter Malnati sat down and poured out his feelings in a journal entry. “I had to get that stuff out of my head,” he said. “I just couldn’t believe how much I had transformed my view of what we were doing.”

Additionally, Malnati is one of the five-player directors who opposed the idea of establishing eight chosen occasions with no cuts and reduced fields for players.

Peter believed that this concept meant that the number of opportunities to play for rank and file.

Additionally, the golfer also argued to reduce some chosen events, but it was not accepted and got shut down.

Later, he did a 180, which helped him turn the vote in favor of the tour’s plan. Moreover, the university where he studied was also obligated to publish his thoughts.

Peter mentioned that he was scared but later felt more optimistic. The golfer mentioned he felt enthusiastic about the sponsor of the PGA Tour, about fans, media, partners, and about every single member.
