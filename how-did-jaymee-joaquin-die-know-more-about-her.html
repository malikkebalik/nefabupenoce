How Did Jaymee Joaquin Die? Know More About Her

Jaymee Joaquin, a well-known actress and TV host, sadly lost her life at the age of 44 on October 18, 2023, following a courageous fight against breast cancer. Her passing has left her loved ones and fans heartbroken.

Who was Jaymee Joaquin?

Born on July 27, 1979, Jaymee Joaquin, also known as Jaymee Wins, was a multi-talented individual. She made a name for herself as an actress and TV host, gaining fame for her role in the game show “Games Uplate Live.” Some of her memorable acting roles include appearances in movies like “My Big Love,” “Kutob,” and “Tanging Ina N’yong Lahat.” But Jaymee’s talents extended beyond the screen; she authored two self-published books, “That Sh*t Called Cancer” and “No-Hair Mama, Don’t Care,” demonstrating her resilience in the face of adversity. She even had a podcast show titled “WIN Your Daily Battles” and was working on an upcoming documentary called “Beautiful Scars.”

Notably, she hosted ABS-CBN’s late-night game show “Games Uplate Live” from 2006 to 2009, becoming an iconic figure in the world of entertainment. Throughout her life, Jaymee embraced adventure, spread joy through her laughter, and radiated love, both personally and through her advocacy work. Her inspiring journey touched the hearts of many and left a lasting legacy.

How Did Jaymee Joaquin Die? What Was Her Cause of Death?

Jaymee Joaquin’s journey with breast cancer began in 2016, when she first detected a small, marble-sized lump in her left breast. She underwent treatment, but unfortunately, the cancer returned in both 2019 and 2020, revealing the tenacity of her battle. Sadly, at the age of 44, Jaymee, who displayed immense courage throughout her battle with breast cancer, passed away last Wednesday. The news of her passing was confirmed by her cousin, Erika Geronimo, who shared the heartbreaking update in a Facebook post on Wednesday, October 19. Jaymee’s story is a reminder of the strength and resilience people can show in the face of such challenges.
