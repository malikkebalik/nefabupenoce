Maya Jama - Lifestyle-Career

Lifestyle & Career: - Jama relocated to London in 2012 to seek a career in the media, first as an actor, then as a TV presenter and fashion model. Jama's original dream had been to become an actress, but she realized that becoming a presenter would fit her better. Davina McCall and June Sarpong were two of her early role models. Jama made her presenting debut as a youngster, hosting JumpOff.weekly TV music video countdown, and then worked for Sky UK on TRACE Sports. Maya's FIFA World Cup Cities for Copa90, a travelogue chronicling the 2014 FIFA World Cup in Brazil, was hosted by her in 2014. She collaborated with Brit Awards in February 2017, hosting their 2017 Pre-Brit Awards Party and a Facebook live stream from the red carpet. Jama co-hosted the debut season of The Circle on Channel 4 with Alice Levine in September 2018. Jama featured as a celebrity guest judge on RuPaul's Drag Race UK's second season in February 2021. Jama was revealed as the host of Simon Cowell's new music competition gameshow Walk The Line in September 2021. Jama was revealed as the new host of the ITV2 dating reality show Love Island in October 2022, succeeding Laura Whitmore.

She presented #DriveWithMaya, a weekday show on Rinse FM, from 2014 to 2017. In 2018, she joined BBC Radio 1, where she co-hosts Radio 1's Greatest Hits on Saturdays and co-hosts Radio 1's Greatest Hits every Friday with Scott Mills and Chris Stark. Jama presented When Life Gives You Melons, a podcast accessible on streaming platforms in collaboration with Freya Lingerie, in 2018. In addition, in June 2018, she created a popular apparel collection with PrettyLittleThing, followed by a second collection in February 2019. Jama debuted her own face and eye masks line, MIJ Masks, in December 2020, with the first release selling out in less than 24 hours.
