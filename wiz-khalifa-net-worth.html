Early Life

Born Cameron Jibril Thomaz on September 8, 1987, in Minot, North Dakota, Wiz Khalifa’s early life unfolded amidst the challenges of his parents’ military service and subsequent divorce.

Moving across continents, from Japan to the U.K. and Germany, Khalifa’s family finally settled in Pittsburgh in 1996. It was here that the young talent, then known as Cameron, began penning and performing his songs, setting the stage for his musical odyssey.

Wiz Khalifa Career

Wiz Khalifa’s ascent in the music realm began with his discovery by Benjy Grinberg, the founder of Rostrum Records, at the age of 16 in 2004.

Wiz Khalifa Career

Khalifa’s discography is a testament to his artistic evolution. From breakthrough hits like “Black and Yellow” to collaborations with Snoop Dogg in “Young, Wild & Free,” his musical journey expanded across genres. Notable albums like “Rolling Papers,” “O.N.I.F.C.,” and “Blacc Hollywood” contributed to Khalifa’s standing as a hip-hop luminary. His recent works, including “Rolling Papers 2” (2018) and “Multiverse” (2022), showcase his enduring influence.

Personal Life

Wiz Khalifa’s personal notes include a marriage to model Amber Rose, joint custody of their son Sebastian Taylor Thomaz, and subsequent relationships. A vocal advocate for marijuana, Khalifa’s alignment with cannabis culture extends to partnerships and sponsorships. His foray into Brazilian jiu-jitsu and Muay Thai adds a martial arts dimension to his multifaceted lifestyle.
