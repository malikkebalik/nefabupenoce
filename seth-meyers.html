Professional Career

Seth began his career as a member of the North-western University improving sketch group “Mee-ow show.” In 2001, Seth auditioned for Saturday Night Live and was recruited as a cast member and writer. In 2005, he ascended to become the show’s writing supervisor.

He rose the ranks to become a co-head writer with Tina Fey, and when Fey left, he was designated the show’s lead writer. He was also the head writer for SNL and acted in the shows. Seth became the lone anchor of the regular sketch after Poehler departed SNL in 2008, a role he kept until 2013 when he left SNL.

While at SNL, he performed, wrote, and impersonated celebrities such as Sean Penn, Prince Charles, John Kerry, and others. He rose to prominence through his acting and writing. Some of his most well-known sketches include the needlers, Abe Lincoln, and Sears’ photographers.

Seth left Saturday Night Live in 2013 to join NBC’s “Late Night with Seth Meyers.” Jimmy Fallon, a friend, and SNL alum held the role. Since 2014, he has presented the show, which includes his hilarious monologues as well as interviews with notable guests. Amy Pohler was his first guest host on the show.

Seth has also been in films such as Journey to the Center of the Earth, Spring Breakdown, Hot Rod, Spin City, and many others.
